Key Insights & Measures to Improve Library Efficiency
1. Popular Books & Authors Drive Demand
Analysis of borrowing frequency revealed top-performing books and authors, indicating strong reader preferences.
Measure: Increase inventory for high-demand titles to reduce wait times and improve availability.

2. Genre-Based Reading Trends
Certain genres show higher borrowing activity compared to others.
Measure: Allocate more budget and shelf space to high-demand genres while optimizing low-performing categories.

3. Borrowing Activity Patterns
Monthly borrowing trends highlight periods of higher library usage.
Measure: Schedule book promotions, reading events, or new releases during high-engagement months.

4. Highly Active Members
A small group of members accounts for a significant share of total borrowings.
Measure: Introduce loyalty programs, extended borrowing privileges, or personalized recommendations for frequent users.

5. Overdue Borrowings Affect Circulation
Some books remain unreturned beyond the expected borrowing period, reducing book availability.
Measure: Implement automated reminders, late penalties, or digital notifications to improve return compliance.

6. Borrow Duration Insights
Average borrow duration indicates typical reading/return behavior among members.
Measure: Adjust borrowing policies or loan duration to balance accessibility and availability.

7. Overall Return Rate Monitoring
Return rate analysis helps track how efficiently borrowed books are returned.
Measure: Regular monitoring ensures better inventory circulation and improved library management.
